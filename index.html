Krishna Karra

I'm a freelance data scientist and engineer focused on earth observation, satellite imagery and remote sensing. I'm also an adjunct professor at The Cooper Union and teach a graduate level class "Fundamentals of Remote Sensing and Earth Observation".

Most recently, I worked with Impact Observatory to create a global land use / land cover map from Sentinel-2 imagery, which you can read more about here . Before that, I worked on a piece in the New York Times visualizing the natural colors of land from aerial imagery, sampled and arranged by political affiliation. The piece won a Malofiej data visualization award and you can read more about it here .

I'm always looking to connect with like-minded folks doing impactful work in this field. Please send me an e-mail to get in touch.
